## 3. Central Question Reframing

**R1 framing:** "Is the exegetical case for restriction sound?" (a question about textual evidence)

**R4 framing:** "What does faithful practice require given irreducible exegetical uncertainty?" (a question about institutional and pastoral obligation under contested evidence)

This reframing was driven by three convergent findings:
1. **The Hermeneutician's consistency test** -- the stated hermeneutical principle does not survive consistent application, moving the debate from "who has the better reading?" toward "what do you do when the reading is genuinely underdetermined?"
2. **The Systematician's imago Dei argument** -- shifted the question from exegetical to theological-internal (the tension is in Alderwood's own doctrinal affirmations)
3. **The Social Analyst's Bourdieu symmetry correction** -- shifted social conditioning from a one-directional challenge into a reflexive diagnostic applicable to all positions

**Rating: REFRAMED.** By R4, all six advocates converge on a question that was not the R1 question: *How should a church hold a restriction when the hermeneutical, exegetical, and theological basis for that restriction is genuinely contested?*

**Limitation:** The reframing is unanimous, raising the question of whether the debate produced genuine disagreement at the level of conclusion. The absence of a strong complementarian advocate means the question was reframed by a panel that lacked a full defense of the position under examination.
