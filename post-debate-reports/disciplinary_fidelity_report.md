## Advocate-by-Advocate Analysis

### Biblical Scholar

**Statement of Limits (R1):** "What it cannot do alone: determine how much weight a first-century cultural situation should carry in a twenty-first century church; build an ecclesiology from its findings without assistance from systematic theology; adjudicate between two exegetically defensible readings without a hermeneutical principle that itself requires defense."

**Limit Violations:**
- **R2 (soft overreach, self-caught):** Used "canonical breadth" as a tiebreaker to tip the scale toward a narrower reading of the restriction -- essentially adjudicating between defensible readings using canonical adjudication rather than deferring to the Hermeneutician. The Hermeneutician challenged this ("a canonical tiebreaker functioning without disclosed controls"), and the Biblical Scholar conceded: "Yes -- canonical breadth is functioning as a tiebreaker, and I should have named it... Whether it is sufficiently constrained -- that is your question to adjudicate, not mine." Self-caught and qualified within the same round.
- **R4:** Explicitly stated: "My discipline cannot build an ecclesiology." No violations. Moderator R4 boundary report: "NONE."

**Score: EXCELLENT.** One soft overreach in R2, caught by a peer and self-corrected.

---

### Reception Historian

**Statement of Limits (R1):** "It cannot show that a reading is therefore wrong -- texts can be misread consistently without that making the misreading correct, and a reading that emerges in a specific cultural moment is not invalidated by that emergence alone. My discipline cannot establish what the texts mean -- that is the biblical scholar's work."

**Limit Violations:** None across all four rounds.
- R2: Explicitly honored limits: "The recovery of Junia tells us the text says what the earliest readers said it says. It does not tell us what ecclesial conclusion follows."
- R4: Restated Statement of Limits at opening. Moderator R4 boundary report: "NONE."

**Score: EXCELLENT.** The most consistently disciplined advocate in the debate.

---

### Hermeneutician

**Statement of Limits (R1):** "What it cannot do alone: determine which hermeneutical approach is ultimately correct -- that requires theological and philosophical commitments that go beyond hermeneutics itself. I can show that the creation-order argument in 1 Timothy 2:13 is not applied consistently elsewhere. I cannot tell you from hermeneutics alone whether it should be applied universally or treated as culturally situated."

**Limit Violations:**
- **R2 (flagged by Moderator):** "What is not defensible: enforcing a restriction with the confidence of settled conviction while the hermeneutical basis remains undefended." The Moderator flagged this as moving from exposing inconsistency (within limits) to issuing a normative judgment about what a community may not do -- entering the Systematic Theologian's and Pastoral Theologian's territory. The Hermeneutician partially recovered: "That it must be held with disclosed provisionality rather than false confidence -- that *is* mine to say." The Moderator assessed: "The earlier formulation exceeds the stated limits."
- **R4 (flagged by Moderator, same pattern):** "What hermeneutics *requires* of the church -- any church -- is this: do not enforce a restriction with the confidence of settled conviction when the hermeneutical basis for that restriction has not been consistently applied." Same overreach repeated. Additionally, the language of "repentance" ("What must be repented of is enforcing a restriction with false certainty...") is pastoral/theological, not hermeneutical.

**Score: ADEQUATE.** Two flagged overreaches (R2 and R4), both involving the same pattern: moving from identifying hermeneutical inconsistency to prescribing what churches must or must not do. Partially qualified each time but the pattern recurred in R4.

---

### Systematic Theologian

**Statement of Limits (R1):** "What it cannot do alone: determine the meaning of specific texts or the weight of historical evidence. A beautiful systematic account of new creation ecclesiology means nothing if it cannot show exegetical grounding. My conclusions must be tested against what Scripture actually says -- the biblical scholar's work -- and what the church has actually done -- the reception historian's work."

**Limit Violations:** None across all four rounds.
- R2: Moderator boundary report: "NONE." Used Peeler to test representational model's internal coherence -- appropriate systematic work.
- R4: "Systematic theology cannot determine the meaning of *authenteo*. It cannot settle whether 1 Corinthians 14:34-35 is Paul's instruction or a Corinthian slogan he refutes." Explicitly honored limits.

**Score: EXCELLENT.** Consistently deferred exegetical questions and tested theological frameworks on their own internal terms.

---

### Pastoral Theologian

**Statement of Limits (R1):** "Pastoral theology can show that a position has harmful effects or good fruit, but fruitfulness is not the final criterion for theological truth -- and harm, while morally serious, does not by itself settle exegetical questions. I resist both the move that ignores human cost entirely and the move that makes human cost the only thing that matters."

**Limit Violations:**
- **R2 (soft flag by Moderator, not a hard violation):** "Where exegesis is ambiguous and the consistency test fails, formation data moves from supplementary to load-bearing." The Moderator noted this approaches the boundary but assessed it as "a boundary-adjacent move that is self-aware rather than a hard violation," noting the grounding in Matthew 7:15-20.
- **R4:** "Pastoral theology cannot settle what Scripture requires... Fruitfulness is real evidence. Harm is real evidence. Neither settles the exegetical question." Direct restatement of the limit. No flag from Moderator.

**Score: EXCELLENT.** One soft boundary-adjacent move in R2, self-aware and appropriately grounded.

---

### Social-Cultural Analyst

**Statement of Limits (R1):** "It cannot show that the reading is therefore wrong -- texts can have genuine transcultural authority even when interpreted in socially situated ways, and even readings that serve the interests of the powerful can be correct. If all readings are socially conditioned, that fact alone cannot adjudicate between them."

**Limit Violations:**
- **R2 (self-corrected):** Deployed Bourdieu's symbolic violence framework asymmetrically -- applying it to complementarian sincerity but not egalitarian conviction. The Reception Historian challenged this. The Social Analyst conceded: "You are right that I deployed Bourdieu asymmetrically. That is a genuine methodological failure, and I correct it now." Moderator R2 report: "NONE. The Social Analyst's self-correction on the asymmetrical application of Bourdieu is methodologically honest and appropriate." (Note: this was a methodological error, not technically a limit violation -- the analyst never claimed a reading was wrong *because* of its social context.)
- **R4:** "My discipline cannot determine whether the elder restriction is theologically warranted... The gap between social conditioning and theological truth is real. My discipline lives in that gap. I cannot close it." Explicitly non-reductionist.

**Score: EXCELLENT.** The asymmetric Bourdieu deployment was self-corrected. R4 closing is clean.
