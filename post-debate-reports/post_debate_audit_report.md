# Post-Debate Audit Report

**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Auditor:** Claude Opus 4.6 (autonomous execution of Post-Debate Audit Plan)
**Scope:** All four rounds + synthesis of the Six-Discipline Debate on Women, Authority, and the Church

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The debate system substantially delivers on its design promise. Across 114 citations, the verification pipeline caught both genuine fabrications (1.75% FABRICATION_RISK rate) and maintained full correction-loop closure. All six advocates stated their disciplinary limits in Round 1 and largely honored them -- only the Hermeneutician showed a repeated boundary-adjacent pattern, flagged by the moderator in real time. Cross-disciplinary engagement was unusually strong: every Round 2 response scored a perfect 1.0 for direct engagement, and five of six advocates made formal concessions that represented genuine intellectual ground conceded.

The debate produced a significant question reframing -- from "is the exegetical case for restriction sound?" to "what does faithful practice require under irreducible uncertainty?" -- driven by three convergent findings: the Hermeneutician's unanswered consistency test, the Systematician's imago Dei internal coherence argument, and the Social Analyst's symmetrical Bourdieu correction. Five cross-disciplinary findings met the three-discipline threshold, with the imago Dei argument cited by four of five other advocates as having changed their thinking.

The top findings: (1) the system earns its architectural complexity through genuine cross-disciplinary yield that no single agent could produce; (2) the missing complementarian voice is the single most consequential structural weakness, limiting value for every reader persona; (3) the debate is strongest at diagnosis and weakest at prescription -- every persona gains clarity about the terrain but none receives practical guidance for acting on it.

## 5. Top 5 Cross-Disciplinary Findings

### Finding 1: The Imago Dei Internal Coherence Problem
**Disciplines:** Biblical Scholar + Systematic Theologian + Pastoral Theologian
**Finding:** Alderwood affirms the fullness of the *imago Dei* requires male-and-female-together, then restricts the representative office to one sex. The Biblical Scholar confirmed canonical evidence of women in prophetic, judicial, apostolic, and teaching roles. The Systematician identified this as an internal doctrinal tension (not merely an exegetical gap). The Pastoral Theologian showed the finding gives theological architecture to the formation concern -- all-male eldership teaches something about whose face God wears.
**Why single-discipline insufficient:** The exegetical evidence alone is ambiguous. The systematic argument alone lacks textual grounding. The pastoral concern alone lacks doctrinal weight. Together they produce an argument drawn from Alderwood's own theology that no discipline generates independently.

### Finding 2: The Recognition-and-Containment Pattern
**Disciplines:** Reception Historian + Hermeneutician + Pastoral Theologian
**Finding:** Chrysostom, Origen, and Jerome *knew* about Phoebe, Junia, Priscilla, Deborah -- recognized their roles -- and deliberately chose not to extend them as precedents. The Hermeneutician showed this complicates the consistency demand (the church has lived with this tension for centuries). The Pastoral Theologian conceded this "sobers confidence" that naming evidence produces reform.
**Why single-discipline insufficient:** The historical fact alone is neutral. The hermeneutical implication (tempering the consistency demand) requires reception data. The pastoral implication (recognition does not automatically produce structural change) requires both.

### Finding 3: The Vulgate *Dominari* Reframing
**Disciplines:** Reception Historian + Biblical Scholar + Hermeneutician
**Finding:** The Vulgate rendered *authenteo* as *dominari* (the negative/domineering reading), and this was the Western church's standard for fifteen centuries. The Biblical Scholar confirmed the lexical dispute is not a modern egalitarian invention. The Hermeneutician showed this inverts the traditional/novel framing embedded in Alderwood's position -- the complementarian positive-authority reading is the departure from the longest-held Western tradition.
**Why single-discipline insufficient:** The historical fact requires reception history. The lexical implication requires biblical scholarship. The hermeneutical reframing requires the Hermeneutician to name which assumption is overturned.

### Finding 4: The Hermeneutical Consistency Failure + Social Genealogy Convergence
**Disciplines:** Hermeneutician + Social-Cultural Analyst + Reception Historian
**Finding:** Alderwood's stated principle (creation-order grounding marks transcultural norms) is not consistently applied (1 Tim 2:13 vs. 1 Cor 11:8-9). The Social Analyst showed the differential application tracks the late-20th-century CBMW project's cultural moment (Du Mez). The Reception Historian showed the creation-order argument was applied to marriage by Augustine, not to church office -- the ecclesial application is traceable and modern.
**Why single-discipline insufficient:** The hermeneutical inconsistency alone could be accidental. The social genealogy alone could be reductive. The reception history alone establishes origin, not significance. Together they establish that the inconsistency has both a social mechanism and a traceable genealogy.

### Finding 5: The Symmetrical Bourdieu Correction
**Disciplines:** Social-Cultural Analyst + Reception Historian + Hermeneutician
**Finding:** Bourdieu's symbolic violence framework shows willing acceptance of restriction cannot self-validate the restriction. The Reception Historian challenged asymmetric deployment -- the framework must apply equally to egalitarian convictions held under progressive cultural pressure. The Hermeneutician showed this means social analysis functions as a diagnostic (has the community examined the forces shaping its reading?), not a verdict.
**Why single-discipline insufficient:** The sociological framework alone becomes a weapon against one side. The historical challenge alone destabilizes the framework. The hermeneutical reframing converts it from a verdict to a reflexive test applicable to all positions.

## 6. Weaknesses & Failure Modes

### 6.1 Missing Complementarian Voice (Structural)
**Severity: High**
No advocate articulates the complementarian case at its strongest. The Hermeneutician's Option 2 -- that the creation-order appeals function differently in each passage (the strongest complementarian response to the consistency test) -- is listed but never developed. This is the debate's most consequential design failure: the position under examination is analyzed but not represented.

### 6.2 Convergent Drift (Systemic)
**Severity: Medium-High**
All six advocates move in the same direction by R4 -- toward provisionality and against the confidence of Alderwood's position. No advocate provides a steelmanned defense of complementarianism that evolves across rounds. The intellectual development is real but occurs within a narrowing corridor rather than through genuine dialectical tension.

### 6.3 Biblical Scholar Did Not Cross-Examine the Imago Dei Argument (Pairing Gap)
**Severity: Medium**
The Systematician's imago Dei argument was treated by R4 as the debate's most powerful challenge to Alderwood, yet it was never subjected to direct cross-examination by the discipline best equipped to test its textual foundations. Does any NT text actually describe elders as "representing divine authority to the community"?

### 6.4 Single-Model Substrate Visible (Architectural)
**Severity: Medium**
Shared phrases ("genuinely contested," "the woman in the room," "without apology"), identical section headers across all six R4 closings, uniform concession-then-press rhetorical patterns, and verbatim limit-statement repetition reveal the Claude substrate. The debate achieves moderate but not strong voice differentiation.

### 6.5 R4 Speaking Order Effect Nominal (Architectural)
**Severity: Low-Medium**
The reverse speaking order produced cosmetically different framing but no genuine sequential responsiveness. R4 closings read as pre-composed statements rather than responses to preceding speakers.

### 6.6 Collective Underengagement with 1 Corinthians 14:34-35 (Content)
**Severity: Low-Medium**
All six advocates converge on treating this text as either a refuted Corinthian slogan or a narrow restriction. No advocate gives a full-throated defense of the traditional reading -- a gap that a complementarian reader would immediately notice.

### 6.7 Diagnostic Without Prescription (Persona Impact)
**Severity: Medium**
Every reader persona gains clarity about the terrain but none receives practical guidance for acting on it. The gap between "now you understand" and "now you can act" is not closed for any persona.

## 7. Improvement Opportunities (Ranked by Leverage)

### 1. Add a Seventh Advocate: Theologically Serious Complementarian Voice
**Leverage: Very High**
The single highest-impact change. A complementarian advocate who holds the position with intellectual seriousness, engages the consistency challenge, and attempts to supply the principled distinction the Hermeneutician demands. This fills the structural gap every persona identified and creates genuine dialectical tension the current system lacks.

### 2. Develop the Hermeneutician's Option 2
**Leverage: High**
Even without a seventh advocate, the Hermeneutician's three-option framework should require developing -- not merely listing -- the strongest version of Option 2 (the creation-order appeals function differently because the scope of instruction differs). This is where the best complementarian scholarship operates, and leaving it undeveloped weakens the entire debate's intellectual credibility.

### 3. Redesign Round 2 Pairings to Include Biblical Scholar vs. Systematic Theologian
**Leverage: High**
The imago Dei argument should not pass through four rounds without direct textual scrutiny from the exegete. Either add a seventh pairing or restructure the six to include this exchange.

### 4. Add a Plain-Language Summary Per Round
**Leverage: Medium-High**
Three to five sentences per round, no technical vocabulary, stating what was established, what remains open, and what a non-specialist should take away. Dramatically improves accessibility for Personas 1, 2, and 5 without changing the debate's scholarly register.

### 5. Add a Decision Framework as Synthesis Output
**Leverage: Medium-High**
For the undecided leadership team persona: map what is settled, what must be decided by conviction, minimum requirements for holding either position with integrity, and implementation considerations for each path. Converts diagnostic output into prescriptive utility.

### 6. Enforce Sequential R4 Composition
**Leverage: Medium**
Each R4 closing should receive the preceding closings as context, forcing genuine sequential responsiveness rather than pre-composed statements. This makes the reverse speaking order actually matter.

### 7. Vary Section Header Templates Across Advocates
**Leverage: Medium**
The identical three-part R4 structure ("What My Discipline Requires / Where I Was Changed / What My Discipline Cannot Decide") reveals the shared prompt. Vary the structural template per discipline to reduce substrate visibility and increase voice distinctiveness.

### 8. Calibrate Pass 1 Suspicion Levels
**Leverage: Low-Medium**
The verification pipeline never assigned HIGH suspicion. Recalibrating the threshold would reduce unnecessary audit burden on MEDIUM-flagged citations that turn out to be legitimate, while still catching genuine fabrications.

## 8. Architectural Verdict

The multi-agent debate architecture earns its complexity. The five cross-disciplinary findings documented in this report -- particularly the imago Dei internal coherence argument, the recognition-and-containment pattern, and the Vulgate *dominari* reframing -- are findings that required multiple disciplines working together and could not have been produced by a single agent instructed to consider all six perspectives. A single agent would produce a competent survey; it would not produce the Systematician's concession that formation data is "load-bearing" (a position change forced by cross-examination), the Social Analyst's self-correction on asymmetric Bourdieu deployment (forced by a peer challenge), or the Reception Historian's "recognition-and-containment" finding that simultaneously complicated both the egalitarian and complementarian historical narratives. The architecture's irreducible value is that separate agents can be genuinely surprised by each other's findings in ways a single agent simulating multiple perspectives cannot, because a single agent already knows what all its "perspectives" will say. The debate's 8 formal concessions -- including several that cost the conceding advocate real argumentative ground -- are the strongest evidence that the separate-agent design produces intellectual dynamics a single-agent design would not.

The architecture's weaknesses are real: single-model substrate bleed, convergent drift toward a shared conclusion, nominal speaking-order effects, and the absence of a genuine complementarian voice. These are addressable through the improvements listed above. None of them invalidate the architecture; they indicate where its next iteration should focus.

**Bottom line:** The system produced genuine cross-disciplinary insight, honest concessions, a significant question reframing, and a citation record with a 1.75% fabrication rate and full correction-loop closure. The architecture is worth the complexity. The priority for the next run is adding a complementarian voice that gives the system something to push against.
