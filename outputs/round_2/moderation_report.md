**Reception Historian:** NONE

**Hermeneutician:** FLAG
The Hermeneutician states: "What is not defensible: enforcing a restriction with the confidence of settled conviction while the hermeneutical basis remains undefended." This moves from exposing hermeneutical inconsistency (within limits) to issuing a normative judgment about what a community *may not* do — which enters the Systematic/Ecclesiological Theologian's territory regarding ecclesial authority and the Pastoral Theologian's territory regarding community obligations. The Hermeneutician's Statement of Limits specifies that determining which hermeneutical approach is ultimately correct requires theological and philosophical commitments beyond hermeneutics itself. Declaring a community's practice "not defensible" is a theological-normative judgment, not a hermeneutical one. The immediately following sentence — "That it must be held with disclosed provisionality rather than false confidence — that *is* mine to say" — partially recovers the boundary by reframing the claim as epistemic rather than ecclesial, but the earlier formulation exceeds the stated limits.

**Systematic/Ecclesiological Theologian:** NONE
The Systematic Theologian's use of Peeler to test the representational model's internal coherence is appropriate systematic work — testing whether a theological warrant yields its claimed conclusion on its own terms. The concession to the Social Analyst regarding social embeddedness is appropriately qualified. No overreach noted.

**Pastoral Theologian:** SOFT FLAG — NOTED, NOT HARD VIOLATION
The Pastoral Theologian states: "I am competing with you — honestly, and with a doctrine I am prepared to defend — when the exegetical case is genuinely open and the pastoral evidence is genuinely weighty." I note this as a boundary-adjacent move that is self-aware rather than a hard violation.
