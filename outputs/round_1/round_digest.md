**Round 1 Digest — Opening Statements**

*One sentence per advocate on their main argument this round:*
- **Biblical Scholar:** The exegetical evidence is genuinely contested at every decisive point — *authenteō*, the Levitical/apostolic precedents, Junia and Phoebe — and does not unambiguously establish either a universal male-only leadership norm or undifferentiated egalitarianism.
- **Reception Historian:** The specific form of complementarianism Alderwood inherits has a traceable genealogy in the late-1980s culture wars, the early epigraphic record attests women in formal ministerial roles, and the document's reading cannot be presented as arising transparently from Scripture.
- **Hermeneutician:** Alderwood's stated hermeneutical principle — creation-order grounding marks transcultural norms — fails the consistency test because Paul uses identical creation-order reasoning in 1 Corinthians 11 for head coverings, which virtually no complementarian applies universally.
- **Systematic Theologian:** Alderwood's position rests on an unexamined tension between a functional account of eldership and a representational argument requiring maleness, and its own affirmation that the *imago Dei* requires male-and-female-together may internally undermine the restriction it defends.
- **Pastoral Theologian:** Regardless of doctrinal intent, the practice of all-male eldership forms congregations to associate ultimate spiritual authority with maleness, and churches maintaining restriction must demonstrate — not merely assert — that their structural accountability and promises to gifted women are actually kept.
- **Social and Cultural Analyst:** Alderwood's framework was shaped by movement architects responding to specific cultural anxieties, and the document cannot distinguish its theology from those social forces without examining its own genealogy.

*Key tension this round:* The hermeneutician's consistency challenge — why creation-order grounding is transcultural in 1 Timothy 2 but not in 1 Corinthians 11 — is unresolved and cuts to the methodological foundation of Alderwood's central argument.
