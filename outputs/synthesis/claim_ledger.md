# CLAIM LEDGER — AFTER ROUND 3

**CLAIM_ID:** BS1
**CLAIM:** "The canon does not say 'leadership belongs to men with these exceptions.' It presents women exercising prophetic, judicial, teaching, and apostolic functions *without apology or qualification* — and then presents restriction texts whose scope is lexically contested."
**SOURCE_AGENT:** Biblical Scholar (Round 2)
**CITATION_STATUS:** UNVERIFIED
**LOAD_BEARING:** YES

**RESPONSES:**
- Systematic/Ecclesiological Theologian: partially affirms (accepts that the representational model's theological warrants may not sustain the gendered conclusion)
- Hermeneutician: partially affirms (consistency test confirms restriction texts are hermeneutically unresolved)
- Reception Historian: affirms (patristic evidence supports women in attested roles without apology)
- Social and Cultural Analyst: no engagement (Rounds 1–2); Round 3: affirms (Romans 16 sociological analysis confirms women held patronal, diaconal, and apostolic roles with real social authority; Deborah/Huldah framing as exceptions is a retrospective construction, not textual)
- Pastoral Theologian: Round 3: affirms (Alderwood's omission of Deborah and Huldah is formatively significant; the canon presents these women without apology and the church's silence about them forms congregations to read the canon through restriction texts only)

**STATUS:** UNRESOLVED
**TYPE:** Exegetical

**CLAIM_ID:** RH1
**CLAIM:** "Eldon Jay Epp documents that the masculinization of Junia to Junias in critical editions began with Aegidius of Rome in the thirteenth century and was cemented in modern critical texts, despite the unanimous witness of patristic commentators who recognized the name as female."
**SOURCE_AGENT:** Reception Historian (Round 2)
**CITATION_STATUS:** UNVERIFIED
**LOAD_BEARING:** YES

**RESPONSES:**
- Biblical Scholar: affirms (canonical breadth argument depends partly on Junia's apostolic status being textually recoverable); Round 3: reaffirms (Epp's grammatical argument that inclusive reading of *en tois apostolois* is more natural than Burer-Wallace reading)
- Hermeneutician: Round 3: affirms (burden of proof falls on restrictive reading; Junia-as-apostle requires no special apparatus, alternative requires inventing unattested masculine name)
- Systematic/Ecclesiological Theologian: Round 3: engages (raises ecclesiological question — if Spirit appointed a woman to apostolic function, what doctrine of authority explains why Spirit cannot appoint a woman to elder function)
- Social and Cultural Analyst: Round 3: affirms (Junia's masculinization is documented case of theological interest shaping textual transmission; boundary between what text says and what interpreters made it say is not always clean)
- Pastoral Theologian: Round 3: affirms (Junia represents most expansive picture of women's ministry in Pauline corpus; Alderwood's failure to engage her systematically is inconsistent with promise of flourishing)

**STATUS:** PARTIALLY_RESOLVED
**TYPE:** Historical
