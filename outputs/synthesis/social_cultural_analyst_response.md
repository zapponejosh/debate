**CALIBRATION RESPONSE — Social and Cultural Analyst**

The synthesis accurately represents my position on the social construction of creation-order rhetoric. One tension deserves sharper framing: the synthesis notes that the "authority" discussion involves disciplines talking past each other, but from my vantage the more important missed point is *asymmetry of self-examination*. Other disciplines were asked whether their evidence settles the question; my discipline was implicitly asked whether social conditioning *disqualifies* a reading. Those are different challenges. The synthesis should note that no advocate — including me — has yet answered whether a position that cannot account for its own social genealogy is epistemically entitled to the confidence it claims. That asymmetry remains live.
