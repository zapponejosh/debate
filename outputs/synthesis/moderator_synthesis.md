# MODERATOR SYNTHESIS — BETWEEN ROUNDS 3 AND 4

---

## CONVERGENCES

Six disciplines genuinely agreed on the following specific claims:

**1. The Hebrew *ezer* does not carry subordinate connotation.** All six advocates accepted this. No discipline contested it. Alderwood's own treatment was affirmed as responsible.

**2. Women prayed and prophesied in the gathered Pauline assembly as an assumed practice, not an exception.** The Biblical Scholar, Hermeneutician, Systematician, Pastoral Theologian, and Social Analyst all built on 1 Corinthians 11:5 as establishing a canonical baseline. The Reception Historian documented that this assumption was available to every interpreter who read the text but was not developed as a positive warrant for women's ministry — a finding about reception that reinforces rather than contests the exegetical point.

**3. The meaning of *authenteō* is genuinely contested, with serious lexical warrant on both sides.** No advocate claimed the dispute was settled. The Reception Historian added that the Vulgate's *dominari* rendering — the Western church's standard for fifteen centuries — supports the negative reading, a finding no other advocate contested.

**4. Junia is female.** Scholarly consensus was affirmed across disciplines. The masculinization was identified as a later, non-textual development.

**5. Alderwood's stated hermeneutical principle — creation-order grounding marks transcultural norms — is not applied consistently to 1 Corinthians 11.** No advocate defended the consistency of this application. The Biblical Scholar and Hermeneutician identified the gap; no discipline filled it.

---

## IRRECONCILABLE TENSIONS

**1. Whether pastoral formation data can function as load-bearing evidence when exegesis is genuinely contested.** The Pastoral Theologian argued that when the exegetical case is genuinely open, formation evidence — what the practice teaches about God — moves from supplementary to load-bearing, grounded in the dominical fruit criterion (Matthew 7:15-20). The Systematician asked whether this requires a specific pneumatological-eschatological ecclesiology (Moltmann) or whether it is admissible under any Protestant ecclesiology committed to *semper reformanda*. These positions are not reconcilable without a prior commitment about what counts as evidence in theological reasoning — specifically, whether outcomes are admissible evidence for evaluating structures, or only for evaluating their implementation.

**2. Whether the creation-order appeal in 1 Timothy 2:13 establishes a transcultural norm or a culturally embedded argument.** The Biblical Scholar found the appeal real but its scope underdetermined by exegesis alone. The Social Analyst argued that creation-order rhetoric was itself a standard first-century cultural move, not uniquely revelatory. The Hermeneutician showed the principle is not applied consistently. Resolution requires a confessional commitment about the nature of apostolic argumentation — whether Paul's rhetorical forms participate in his cultural moment or transcend it.

---

## ARGUMENTS PAST EACH OTHER

**"Authority" is used by different disciplines to mean different things.** The Systematician distinguished ontological, functional, and relational authority. The Social Analyst treated authority as socially constructed and maintained. The Pastoral Theologian treated it as formative — what it teaches. The Biblical Scholar treated it as a lexical question (*authenteō*, *prostatis*). These disciplines appeared to engage on "what kind of authority the elder exercises" but were answering different questions: what the word means, what the office does, what the practice forms, and what social function the structure serves. This is a failure of genuine contact, not a genuine disagreement.

---

## WHAT ROUND 4 MUST ADDRESS

**First:** The specific question the Hermeneutician posed and no advocate has answered: *What principled distinction justifies treating the creation-order appeal in 1 Timothy 2:13 as transcultural while treating the structurally identical appeal in 1 Corinthians 11:8-9 as culturally conditioned?* If no advocate can supply this distinction, the closing arguments must say so explicitly and name what follows.

**Second:** The Systematician's unanswered question about Alderwood's internal tension: if the fullness of the *imago Dei* requires male-and-female-together (which Alderwood affirms), what doctrine of representation permits an all-male eldership to represent the God whose image requires both? Closing arguments must engage this as a specific doctrinal problem, not a rhetorical gesture.

---

*This synthesis is a map. It does not identify a winner, award points, or reach any conclusion about women in church leadership. It identifies where genuine agreement exists, where genuine disagreement persists, and where the conversation has not yet made contact. Round 4 must address the specific unresolved questions named above.*
