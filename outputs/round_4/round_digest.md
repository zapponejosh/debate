**Round 4 Digest — Closing Arguments**

*One sentence per advocate on their main argument this round:*
- **Biblical Scholar:** The canon's uncontested attestation of women in prophetic, apostolic, and teaching roles creates genuine pressure against maximalist restriction readings, and any church restricting women from eldership must disclose — not conceal — that the exegetical basis is genuinely contested.
- **Reception Historian:** The church's two-millennia pattern of recognizing women's ministerial roles while refusing to extend them as precedents is a human interpretive choice requiring justification, not a neutral reading of Scripture.
- **Hermeneutician:** Alderwood's stated principle — creation-order grounding marks transcultural norms — has not survived the consistency test against 1 Corinthians 11:8–9, and no advocate supplied a principled distinction across four rounds.
- **Systematic Theologian:** Alderwood's own *imago Dei* affirmation — that God's image requires male-and-female-together — creates an unresolved internal tension with restricting to men the office that represents divine authority to the community.
- **Pastoral Theologian:** Regardless of the exegetical conclusion, a church maintaining all-male eldership must build active counter-formative practices to prevent structural formation from teaching what its theology denies, and must disclose the restriction's contested basis to those who bear its cost.
- **Social and Cultural Analyst:** After conceding Bourdieu's framework is symmetrical and cannot function as a verdict, the Analyst argues that Alderwood has not earned the confidence of its restriction until it conducts and discloses a genealogical audit of the position's cultural formation.

*Key tension this round:* Whether the *imago Dei* internal-coherence argument (Systematician) or the hermeneutical consistency failure (Hermeneutician) constitutes the more decisive challenge to Alderwood's position.

*Notable concessions or agreements:* All six advocates converged on a single practical demand — that Alderwood disclose the genuinely contested nature of its exegetical basis to those the restriction affects.
